Something About Me

I am currently graduating in IT data analytics at NSCC. With a background in Mathematics, I started my career as a project engineer at a hard disk drive manufacturing company where I used JMP to build regression models that predicted hard disk yield.

My ability to consolidate different sources of data and manipulate them provided valuable insights to the management, ultimately saving the cost of production.

I then worked in an international bank where I supported bankers with creating charts and presentations using Microsoft Office. In 2011, I earned a master's degree in data science and business statistics and moved on to work as a business analyst at a construction equipment company. There, I prepared management statistics reports from historical sales data, customer trends, calculated the product price, and worked on yearly budget. I also worked on user acceptance tests with the IT department, showcasing my analytical and technical skills.
